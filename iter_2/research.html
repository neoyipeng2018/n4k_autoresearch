Concise research findings on iter_2/

iter_2 is a self-contained autonomous macro strategy search workflow. Its fixed engine tests retail ETF rotation strategies, rejects account-ruin cases, and ranks surviving experiments solely by CAGR. The current best implementation is a highly concentrated, monthly rebalanced, 21-trading-day momentum selector over a thematic/risk-on ETF universe.

What the workflow does

  • Objective: maximize CAGR without ruin. Ruin is a hard disqualifier; among non-ruined strategies, only CAGR matters.
  • Benchmark: DBMF is mandatory and matched to the same date window, but it is diagnostic only—not the ranking target.
  • Data: daily Yahoo adjusted-close prices are read from ~/.cache/macroresearch/market/*.csv, with direct Yahoo fetching as fallback.
  • Experiment loop: modify train.py only, run mypy/ruff, execute uv run train.py > run.log 2>&1, parse metrics, keep only CAGR-improving non-ruined variants, append results.tsv.
  • Analysis: analysis.ipynb / analysis.executed.ipynb summarize the ledger and generate progress.png.

Core engine specifics in prepare.py

AreaSpecific behavior
Universe + benchmarkStrategy symbols are supplied by train.py; DBMF is always added and required.
Backtest cadenceSignals are evaluated at the start of each new calendar month using data only through the prior trading day, reducing lookahead risk.
ReturnsDaily simple returns from adjusted closes; missing/infinite returns become 0.0.
Weight validationWeights below -1 are rejected; absolute single weight and total gross exposure are capped at 2.0. Empty valid weights fall back to SGOV if available.
Ruin ruleRuin if equity is invalid, non-finite, non-positive, or ever ≤ 0.25.
CAGRCalendar-day CAGR from first to last equity point, not 252-day annualization.

Current strategy in train.py

The live best strategy is thematic top-1 one-month momentum:

SYMBOLS = QQQ, XLK, XLY, XLE, MTUM, QUAL, HYG, GLD, DBC, TLT, SMH, SOXX, XLC, XBI, SGOV
MOMENTUM_DAYS = 21
TOP_N = 1
RISK_CAP = 1.0
TREND_DAYS = 0
USE_BLEND_SCORE = False
USE_VOL_ADJUSTED_SCORE = False
USE_SCORE_WEIGHTS = False
USE_SPY_GUARD = False
  • At each monthly rebalance, compute each non-cash ETF's trailing 21-trading-day return.
  • Buy the single highest-scoring ETF at 100% weight.
  • If no score is available or the best score is non-positive, hold SGOV.
  • The strategy is long-only in practice, although the engine supports limited negative weights.

Empirical findings from results.tsv

  • Best: experiment 38, thematic_top1_1m_momentum, CAGR 0.343744, excess vs DBMF 0.249840, non-ruined.
  • The largest improvement came from adding semiconductors/communication/biotech thematic exposure to the fast top-1 momentum setup.
  • Fast lookbacks dominated slower ones: 1m/21d/30d variants were consistently near the top.
  • Concentration helped more than diversification: top-1 generally beat top-2/top-3; score weighting reduced the best result.
  • Vol-adjustment and broad/global additions were weak in this run; the worst ledger result was sector_top1_voladj_6m at CAGR 0.031943.
  • No logged experiment crashed or triggered the formal ruin threshold.

Important caveats

  • Overfitting risk: the ledger shows iterative search over many ETF sets/lookbacks on one historical window; the winning thematic/AI exposure may be sample-specific.
  • Survivorship and availability: Yahoo adjusted closes are convenient but not a full institutional data pipeline; ETF inception dates and missing data matter.
  • Ruin is narrow: formal ruin only means equity ≤ 25% or invalid values. It does not penalize drawdown, turnover, taxes, slippage, liquidity, concentration, or future regime fragility.
  • Monthly timing specificity: signals use prior-day data at month changes, but executions assume frictionless daily close-to-close returns after rebalance.
