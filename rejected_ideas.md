# Rejected Ideas

## Cycle 1 — 2026-05-26 / may27-cross-asset

### Reject: Long broad commodities after the oil shock

- Asset class: commodities
- Candidate expression: Long `DBC` or oil futures / `USO`.
- Theory anchor: inflation impulse / commodity supply-demand.
- Reason for rejection: The macro narrative is plausible but the spot commodity leg has already moved sharply: WTI was up roughly 72.6% over trailing 252 trading observations and broad commodities were up about 30.5% year over year. Without curve/inventory data and roll analysis, this risks chasing the first-order move. A cleaner second-order expression is inflation compensation rather than outright commodity beta.
- What would change the decision: Evidence that futures curves, inventories, and positioning remain supportive and that roll/carry is favorable.

### Reject: Short high-yield credit on higher rates and oil inflation

- Asset class: credit
- Candidate expression: Short `HYG` / `JNK` or CDX HY conceptually.
- Theory anchor: liquidity / credit / risk appetite.
- Reason for rejection: The logic is plausible because real yields and oil have risen, but credit spreads are currently tight and improving: HY OAS was 2.74 as of 2026-05-25 and had compressed over the past quarter. Short credit has negative carry and requires stronger evidence of funding stress or default deterioration. The current data shows easy financial conditions rather than confirmed credit stress.
- What would change the decision: HY OAS widening, NFCI tightening, lending standards worsening, or labor data weakening.

### Watchlist but not developed: USDJPY upside from US/Japan rate divergence

- Asset class: FX / rates
- Candidate expression: Long USDJPY or long USD versus JPY basket.
- Theory anchor: real-rate differential / monetary policy divergence.
- Reason not developed: USDJPY was already high at 158.69 as of 2026-05-15. Carry may support the trade, but intervention and crowding risk are material. Without current positioning and intervention-risk analysis, this is not the best ignored-by-smart-money candidate.
- What would change the decision: Cleaner evidence that positioning is not crowded and policy intervention risk is lower than priced.

### Watchlist but not developed: US 5s30s or 2s10s steepener on fiscal/term-premium risk

- Asset class: rates
- Candidate expression: 5s30s steepener or long 2y/short 10y duration equivalent.
- Theory anchor: fiscal impulse / term premium / monetary policy reaction function.
- Reason not developed: Long-end yields are high and the curve has flattened recently, so fiscal/term-premium logic is plausible. But this run lacks Treasury issuance/refunding data and positioning. The breakeven idea better isolates the observable inflation/commodity shock.
- What would change the decision: Updated Treasury issuance data, auction tails, term-premium estimates, and curve-positioning evidence.
